If you're losing business to voicemail, you've got two real options: a traditional **answering service** (a call centre with human agents) or a modern **AI receptionist**. Both answer your phone when you can't — but they're very different in cost, speed, and how they feel to your customer.

## Traditional answering services

A live-agent service has real people answering under your business name. Upsides: it's human, and it's been around forever. Downsides:

- **Cost:** typically billed per minute or per call — it adds up fast at volume.
- **Scripts:** agents follow a generic script and often know little about your business.
- **Hold times:** at busy moments, your caller waits.

## AI receptionists

An [AI receptionist](/ai-receptionist) is trained specifically on your business — your services, your hours, your FAQs. Upsides:

- **Always instant:** it answers on the first ring, 24/7, and never puts anyone on hold.
- **Flat, predictable pricing** instead of per-minute meters.
- **Books directly** into your calendar and texts you a transcript.

The honest downside: it's software, so it has guardrails — anything it can't handle gets passed to you as a message. That's by design, not a bug.

## How to choose

- Want a warm human voice and don't mind per-call costs? A live answering service may fit.
- Want instant answers, flat pricing, real booking, and a setup that actually knows your business? The AI receptionist wins for most local operators.

For a growing business in [Brampton](/locations/brampton) or across the GTA, the AI receptionist usually delivers more captured leads per dollar — which is the only number that matters.
