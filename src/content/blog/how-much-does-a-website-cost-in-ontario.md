Short version: a professional small-business website in Ontario in 2026 runs anywhere from about **$500 to $15,000+**, and most local businesses land between **$1,000 and $3,500**. That's a huge range, so let's break down what actually moves the number.

## What you're really choosing between

There are three tiers, and they solve different problems:

- **DIY builders (Wix, Squarespace, ~$20/mo):** cheapest up front, but you pay in time, and the result usually looks like a template because it is one. Fine for a placeholder, weak for ranking or converting.
- **Freelancer ($800–$2,500):** a real custom site from one person. Quality varies wildly — vet the portfolio and ask who answers when something breaks.
- **Agency ($3,000–$15,000+):** more process, more people, more polish — and often more overhead you're paying for. Downtown Toronto agencies sit at the top of this range.

## What drives the price

1. **Number of pages** — a 5-page site is very different from a 30-page one.
2. **Custom design vs template** — bespoke design and animation cost more than a themed theme.
3. **Functionality** — booking, e-commerce, and [AI tools like an AI receptionist](/ai-receptionist) add scope.
4. **SEO and content** — a site built to actually rank with [local SEO](/services) is worth more than a pretty brochure nobody finds.
5. **Who you're dealing with** — a solo builder has less overhead than an agency with account managers.

## What's fair for a local business

For most trades, clinics, and shops across the GTA — say a business in [Brampton](/locations/brampton) or [Mississauga](/locations/mississauga) — a fast, modern, search-ready site in the **$997–$2,500** range is the sweet spot. Above that, you should be getting real custom design, serious SEO, or added tools, not just a bigger invoice.

## The real cost is a bad website

The most expensive website is the one that loads slowly, looks dated, and quietly sends customers to your competitor. Whatever you spend, spend it on speed, search visibility, and turning visitors into calls — not decoration.
