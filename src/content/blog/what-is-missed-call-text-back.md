You're on a ladder, driving, or already with a customer. The phone rings, you can't get it, and it goes to voicemail. Here's the brutal part: **most people won't leave a voicemail.** They just call the next business on the list. That missed call was a real job, and it's gone.

## What it actually does

The moment a call to your business goes unanswered, the caller automatically gets a friendly text from you — within seconds. Something like:

> "Hi! Sorry we missed your call — this is Dave's Plumbing. How can we help?"

Now, instead of silence, there's a conversation. The lead replies, you answer when you get a moment, and the job is saved. It runs on your existing business number and only kicks in when a call goes unanswered.

## Who it's for

Any business where the phone is the lifeline and you can't always answer it:

- Trades and home services
- Clinics and salons
- Restaurants and shops

If you've ever spotted a missed call an hour late and wondered if it was a customer — this is for you.

## Text-back vs a full AI receptionist

Text-back is the simple, entry-level version — it catches the lead with a text. An [AI receptionist](/ai-receptionist) goes further: it actually **answers** the call, holds a conversation, and books the appointment live. Many businesses start with text-back and upgrade once they see how many leads they were losing.
